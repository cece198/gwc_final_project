ABOUT US

Cecelia

      She is 17 years old and going into her senior year of High School. She lives in Staten Island, New York and attends Notre Dame Academy High School. She holds different positions in various extra curricular activities including, but not limited to: President of the Art Club, Varsity Swimmer, member of the National Honor Society, member of the Science National Honor Society, member of the Photography Club, Girl Scout Gold Award recipient, and Co-President of Green Angels . In freshman year of high school, she joined a club called Green Angels. Green Angels is an environmental awareness club that tackles the issues of global warming and overproduction of waste. Her passion for helping the environment and the photography collection called "Plastic Trees" by Eduardo Leal inspired her to create this game.
      Her interest in technology sparked in her Junior year of high school. For her Gold Award project in Girl Scouts, she utilized education and social media to reach out to a vast audience. While she was working on it, she developed the desire to learn how applications are built and how social media works. She learned about the gender gap in STEM through an article on the Girl Scouts' website, which is also where she found out about Girls Who Code. Girls Who Code has encouraged her to pursue a profession in Computer Science and have the confidence to be one of the only women in a male dominated field.

    Regan

          Regan is 17 years old and grew up on Long Island. She is a senior at Kellenberg Memorial High School. At her high school, Regan is an active member of the auditorium and morning announcements production crew, an editor of the literary magazine (The Renaissance), and designs lighting scenes for all of the high school drama productions. Regan is a math tutor and a member of the National Honor Society.
          During her Freshman year of high school, Regan joined the morning announcements crew and the computer club. She spends her mornings either behind the camera or going from classroom to classroom fixing teachers computer's and projector's. Her love of technology and its different branches lead her to apply for Girls Who Code during her Junior year of high school. Being one of two girls on the technical services team at school, the advancement of women in the technology field is a topic that Regan feels strongly about. Regan plans to study Television and Media Production when she attends college in the fall of 2016.
